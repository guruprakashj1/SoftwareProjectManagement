Scrum Terminologies

Interactive Scrum Glossary

Click on each term to learn more about it:

Sprint

A sprint is a time-boxed iteration in Scrum, typically lasting 1-4 weeks, during which a usable and potentially releasable product increment is created.

Key characteristics:

  • Fixed duration that cannot be shortened or lengthened
  • Goal is defined at the sprint planning meeting
  • No changes are made that would endanger the sprint goal
  • Scope may be clarified and re-negotiated between the Product Owner and Development Team

Product Backlog

An ordered list of everything that is known to be needed in the product, maintained by the Product Owner.

Features:

  • Dynamic and constantly evolving
  • Prioritized by business value
  • Contains user stories, features, requirements, enhancements, and fixes
  • Items at the top are more refined than those at the bottom

Sprint Backlog

The set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint, plus a plan for delivering them.

Components:

  • Selected Product Backlog items
  • Tasks needed to deliver those items
  • At least one process improvement identified in the previous Retrospective
  • Owned by the Development Team

Sprint Review

A meeting held at the end of the Sprint to inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed.

Purpose:

  • Inspect the work completed during the sprint
  • Collaborate on what to do next
  • Adjust the Product Backlog based on feedback
  • Time-boxed to 1 hour per week of sprint (e.g., 2 hours for a 2-week sprint)

Retrospective

An opportunity for the Scrum Team to inspect itself and create a plan for improvements to be enacted during the next Sprint.

Typical agenda:

  • What went well during the sprint?
  • What could be improved?
  • Action items for improvement in the next sprint
  • Time-boxed to 45 minutes per week of sprint (e.g., 1.5 hours for a 2-week sprint)

Roles in Scrum

Scrum Role Explorer

Click on each role to see responsibilities and characteristics:

Product Owner

The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing the value of the product resulting from work of the Development Team.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Clearly expressing Product Backlog items
  • Ordering the items in the Product Backlog to best achieve goals and missions
  • Optimizing the value of the work the Development Team performs
  • Ensuring the Product Backlog is visible, transparent, and clear
  • Ensuring the Development Team understands items in the Product Backlog

Scrum Master

The Scrum Master is responsible for promoting and supporting Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Ensuring Scrum is understood and enacted
  • Removing impediments to the Development Team's progress
  • Facilitating Scrum events when needed
  • Coaching the Development Team in self-organization
  • Helping the Product Owner with techniques for effective Product Backlog management

Development Team

The Development Team consists of professionals who do the work of delivering a potentially releasable Increment of "Done" product at the end of each Sprint.

Characteristics:

  • Self-organizing - no one tells them how to turn Product Backlog items into Increments
  • Cross-functional - collectively have all skills needed to create a product Increment
  • No titles - no sub-teams, regardless of domains needing to be addressed
  • Accountable as a whole for delivering work each Sprint
  • Typically 3-9 members

Scrum Team Dynamics

The Scrum Team consists of a Product Owner, the Development Team, and a Scrum Master. Scrum Teams are self-organizing and cross-functional. Self-organizing teams choose how best to accomplish their work, rather than being directed by others outside the team. Cross-functional teams have all competencies needed to accomplish the work without depending on others not part of the team.

Best Practices of Scrum

Daily Scrum Tips

  • Time-box: Keep it to 15 minutes, no matter the team size
  • Stand up: Literally stand to keep energy high and meeting short
  • Three questions: Focus on "What did I do yesterday?", "What will I do today?", "What impediments do I have?"
  • Problem-solving: Take discussions offline with only relevant people

Sprint Planning Advice

  • Ensure the Product Owner comes prepared with a prioritized backlog
  • Break down large items into smaller, manageable pieces
  • Don't overcommit - it's better to underpromise and overdeliver
  • Define a clear sprint goal that guides decision-making

Retrospective Techniques

  • Vary the format to keep it fresh (e.g., Start-Stop-Continue, Mad-Sad-Glad, Sailboat)
  • Focus on actionable improvements, not just complaints
  • Follow up on action items from previous retrospectives
  • Create a safe space for honest feedback

General Scrum Best Practices

  • Maintain a sustainable pace (no heroics)
  • Keep work visible (physical or digital task board)
  • Limit work in progress to focus on completing items
  • Inspect and adapt regularly
  • Focus on delivering value, not just completing tasks
