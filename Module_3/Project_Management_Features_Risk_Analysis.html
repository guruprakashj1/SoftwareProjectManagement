Project Management Features: Risk Analysis

Course Code: CSE4033 | Module 3.1 | Duration: 1.5 Hours

This section focuses on identifying, assessing, and managing risks in IT projects to ensure successful project outcomes.

Understanding Project Risk Analysis

Risk analysis is the process of identifying and assessing factors that may jeopardize the success of a project or prevent it from achieving its objectives. It includes:

  • Risk Identification: Recognizing potential risks before they occur
  • Risk Assessment: Evaluating the probability and impact of identified risks
  • Risk Prioritization: Determining which risks need immediate attention
  • Risk Response Planning: Developing strategies to address risks
  • Risk Monitoring: Tracking identified risks and identifying new ones

Risk Analysis Process

1. Risk Identification

Common techniques include:

  • Brainstorming sessions with project team
  • Expert interviews
  • Checklist analysis
  • SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
  • Review of historical project data

2. Risk Assessment

Evaluate each risk based on:

  • Probability: Likelihood of the risk occurring (Low, Medium, High)
  • Impact: Effect on project objectives if risk occurs (Low, Medium, High)

4. Risk Response Strategies

  • Avoid: Change plans to eliminate the risk
  • Mitigate: Reduce probability or impact
  • Transfer: Shift risk to third party (e.g., insurance)
  • Accept: Acknowledge risk without action

Case Study: Software Development Project

Scenario: Your team is developing a new mobile banking app with a 6-month deadline. Key risks identified:

  1. Third-party payment gateway API might not be ready on time (Probability: Medium, Impact: High)
  2. New security regulations might be introduced (Probability: Low, Impact: High)
  3. Key developer might leave the project (Probability: Medium, Impact: Medium)
  4. App store approval might take longer than expected (Probability: High, Impact: Low)

Exercise: For each risk, suggest appropriate response strategies.
