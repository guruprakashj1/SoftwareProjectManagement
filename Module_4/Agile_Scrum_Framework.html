What is Scrum?

Scrum is an Agile framework for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products. It is:

  • Lightweight: Simple to understand but difficult to master
  • Iterative: Work is organized in fixed-length iterations called sprints
  • Incremental: Builds products piece by piece, with each piece adding value
  • Empirical: Based on observation and experimentation rather than detailed upfront planning

The Scrum Framework

Scrum consists of three main components:

Roles

Product Owner: Maximizes product value and manages the Product Backlog

Scrum Master: Ensures Scrum is understood and enacted, removes impediments

Development Team: Cross-functional group that delivers increments

Artifacts

Product Backlog: Ordered list of everything needed in the product

Sprint Backlog: Set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint

Increment: Sum of all Product Backlog items completed during a Sprint

Events

Sprint: Time-boxed iteration (typically 2-4 weeks)

Sprint Planning: Plan work for the upcoming Sprint

Daily Scrum: 15-minute daily coordination meeting

Sprint Review: Demonstrate work to stakeholders

Sprint Retrospective: Reflect on process improvements

Scrum in Depth

The Sprint Cycle

A Sprint is the heartbeat of Scrum, typically lasting 1-4 weeks. During a Sprint:

  1. Planning: Team selects items from Product Backlog and creates Sprint Backlog
  2. Execution: Daily Scrums help coordinate work and remove impediments
  3. Review: Team demonstrates completed work to stakeholders
  4. Retrospective: Team reflects on how to improve processes

The cycle then repeats with the next Sprint.
