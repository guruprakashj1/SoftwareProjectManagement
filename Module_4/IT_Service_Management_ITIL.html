What is ITIL?

ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) is a framework of best practices for delivering IT services. ITIL aligns IT services with business needs through:

  • Standardized Processes: Defined, repeatable procedures
  • Service Lifecycle Approach: End-to-end service management
  • Continuous Improvement: Regular assessment and refinement
  • Business Focus: Aligning IT with organizational objectives

ITIL Evolution

ITIL has evolved through several versions:

  • ITIL v1 (1989-1999): Initial collection of best practices
  • ITIL v2 (2001): Process-oriented approach
  • ITIL v3 (2007): Service lifecycle framework
  • ITIL 4 (2019): Modernized with Agile/DevOps integration

ITIL 4 introduces the Service Value System (SVS) and focuses on co-creating value.

ITIL Service Lifecycle

Service Strategy

Defines organizational objectives and how IT services support them. Key processes:

  • Strategy management
  • Service portfolio management
  • Financial management
  • Demand management

Service Design

Designs services to meet business requirements. Key processes:

  • Service catalog management
  • Service level management
  • Capacity management
  • Availability management

Service Transition

Manages changes and transitions to production. Key processes:

  • Change management
  • Release management
  • Knowledge management
  • Service asset management

Service Operation

Delivers and manages services daily. Key processes:

  • Incident management
  • Problem management
  • Event management
  • Request fulfillment

Continual Service Improvement

Identifies and implements improvements. Key processes:

  • Service measurement
  • Service reporting
  • Seven-step improvement process

Key ITIL Practices

Essential ITIL Practices

Change Management

Standardized methods for handling changes to minimize impact

Incident Management

Restore normal service operation quickly

Problem Management

Identify and eliminate root causes of incidents

Service Desk

Single point of contact for users

Configuration Management

Maintain information about configuration items

Service Level Management

Define, agree, and manage service levels

IT Asset Management

Manage lifecycle of IT assets

Knowledge Management

Maintain and share organizational knowledge

ITIL vs. DevOps

Aspect ITIL DevOps
Focus Stability, reliability, control Speed, agility, automation
Approach Process-oriented Practice-oriented
Change Frequency Planned, controlled Continuous, automated
Primary Goal Service quality and compliance Rapid delivery and innovation
Complementary ITIL 4 integrates DevOps concepts DevOps benefits from ITIL governance
