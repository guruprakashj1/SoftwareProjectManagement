Introduction to Agile Project Management

Agile Project Management represents a fundamental shift from traditional project management approaches, focusing on flexibility, customer collaboration, and delivering working software frequently. In this module, we'll explore how Agile methodologies help teams adapt to changing requirements and deliver value more effectively in IT projects.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Understand the core principles and values of Agile methodologies
  • Compare Agile approaches with traditional project management
  • Explain the relationship between Agile, Scrum, Lean, and DevOps
  • Identify when Agile approaches are most appropriate for IT projects
  • Recognize various Agile methodologies beyond Scrum

Key Topics

  • Agile Fundamentals: The Agile Manifesto, principles, and values
  • Agile Methodologies: Overview of different Agile approaches
  • Agile in IT: Application of Agile to software development and IT service management
  • Agile Frameworks: Relationship between Scrum, Lean, DevOps, and ITIL
  • Other Agile Methods: Introduction to XP (Extreme Programming), FDD (Feature-Driven Development), DSDM (Dynamic Systems Development Method), and Crystal methodologies

Why Agile Matters in IT Project Management

Traditional "waterfall" approaches often struggle with the dynamic nature of IT projects where requirements frequently change. Agile provides a framework for embracing change, delivering value incrementally, and maintaining alignment with business needs throughout the project lifecycle.

In this module, we'll examine how Agile methodologies have revolutionized IT project management, particularly in software development, and how they integrate with modern practices like DevOps.
